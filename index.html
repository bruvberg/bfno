Why it matters

Five reasons. Une mission.

01
The Language Problem

French institutions do not speak the same French. Legal, administrative, notarial, archival — different registers, different conventions. A bilingual operator translates not just words but precision. The wrong register signals you don’t belong in the room.

02
The System Problem

France has parallel bureaucracies, each with its own logic. A property sale alone touches the notaire, the géomètre, the diagnostician, the cadastre, the mairie. Knowing which door opens which room, and in what order, is most of the work.

03
The Relationship Problem

French business is conducted through introductions. A cold approach reliably fails — not because the French are unfriendly, but because trust is established through networks. Brian is already inside those networks.

04
The Presence Problem

Much of what matters in France requires someone to be there physically. The archive that will not send scans. The notaire who needs signatures in person. The artisan who responds only to someone who shows up. Brian shows up. That sentence does more work than it appears to.

05
The Cultural Problem

French institutions operate in registers that foreigners routinely misjudge. Too formal and the request gets lost. Too casual and the authority collapses. Getting the tone right is invisible work. When it goes wrong, nothing moves.
